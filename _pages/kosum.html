Abstract

KoSum is a Korean YouTube benchmark for video summarization and highlight detection, built from recent videos uploaded between 2024 and 2026. Instead of treating editing as simple cut-based automation, this project connects Most-Replayed viewer engagement signals with visual, audio, and text analysis to generate practical editing guidance. KoSum contains 700 videos across 14 fine-grained content categories and supports triple-modality analysis using visual features, subtitles, audio features, and Most-Replayed importance scores.

Using a triple-modality model, we analyze highlight regions through modality attention patterns and feature-level statistics such as shot transitions, motion dynamics, subtitle density, reaction cues, speech dynamics, and audio novelty. These category-specific findings are organized into analysis cards and used in a data-analysis-conditioned prompting framework, allowing an LLM to generate editing guidelines that are more specific, multimodally aware, and aligned with viewer engagement.
